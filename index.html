Power BI Insurance Brokerage

This project explores the intersection of law, data analytics, and insurance by simulating the operations of a commercial insurance brokerage specializing in construction projects. Given the industry's complexity—marked by regulatory demands, high risk exposure, and contract-heavy operations—I aimed to demonstrate how data-driven solutions can enhance decision-making and client advisory services.

To design a realistic, end-to-end data analytics solution that supports smarter risk assessment, compliance monitoring, and strategic insights for an insurance brokerage environment.

I developed a synthetic dataset using Python in Google Colab, simulating core aspects of a brokerage’s operations. This included data on: Insurance policies and client portfolios; Claims history and claim outcomes; Risk ratings based on project size, duration, and legal/regulatory exposure; Client sectors within the construction industry.

Using Python, I cleaned, transformed, and structured the dataset to mirror real-world insurance workflows, enabling meaningful analysis across time, regions, and client types.

I built an interactive Power BI dashboard that visualizes: Key performance indicators; Trends in claims and policy uptake; Risk profiles by construction project type or region; Regulatory compliance indicators.

The final product demonstrates how legal understanding combined with analytics can uncover actionable insights—such as identifying high-risk clients, optimizing underwriting decisions, and tracking regulatory thresholds.

Skills & Tools Used: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Google Colab); Power BI (DAX, data modeling, dashboard design); Legal and regulatory knowledge applied to data simulation; Domain knowledge in insurance and construction sectors.

With a background in law and a growing specialization in data analytics, I see immense value in bridging these fields. This project not only reflects my technical capabilities but also my interest in building data solutions for industries where regulation, risk, and strategic insight go hand in hand.

Power BI

This project involved analyzing the responses of over 4000 American participants who took part in a blind coffee taste test. The aim was to understand the preferences of the audience and determine which coffee was rated the best.

To ensure the accuracy of the data, I used Power Query to clean and organize it logically. I renamed some columns and words, trimmed and sorted certain value columns, and normalized three columns with "true" and "false" values to simplify and expedite data exploration. I also developed graphic analysis and chose less complex charts, such as pies and bars.

In the middle of the project, I decided to use the decomposition tree as an option for more in-depth analysis to obtain answers to a logical sequence of questions. I explored the graphs provided by Microsoft and uploaded them from the store to Power BI. If you need to answer a logical sequence of questions, this is a good solution for your analysis.

Finally, I utilized Figma to create an aesthetically pleasing layout for the project.

Power BI

The project aimed to analyze historical data to guide a company in developing the next advertising campaign that would have a significant impact on the sports market. The focus was on analyzing Super Bowl advertisements from 2000 to 2021. Rather than just looking at 2022, I conducted a comprehensive analysis.

I looked at the advertising spending patterns of different brands over several years. An obstacle I faced was that each brand had multiple advertisements within a year. To gain a better understanding, I needed to determine how much a specific brand had spent in a single year compared to the average expenditure across all years. To address this, I used DAX functions in Power BI to create measures for obtaining the necessary insights. First, I calculated the total estimated cost for advertisements across all brands for the entire period to get an overall picture. Then, I used distinct count aggregation methods to separate the expenses for each year, enabling a comparison against the average expenditure.

This approach showed that DAX is a powerful tool that can offer valuable insights and perform complex calculations.
